Community Health Integrated Platform(CHIP) V2.0

Streamlining Healthcare Operations at the Grassroots

Project Overview

CHIP V2.0 is a healthcare software developed by Khushi Baby NGO to digitize and manage public health operations at the last mile. The platform includes modules for Login, Attendance (Punch-IN), User Profiles, Digital Health Survey (DHS) Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and critical health programs like ABHA linkage, Family Planning, and tracking Maternal and Infant Mortality (IMR/MMR).

Problem Statement

Context & Background

CHIP is accessed by healthcare workers across a three-tiered hierarchy:

  • Village Level: ASHA workers
  • Sub-Health Center (SHC) Level: ANMs and CHOs
  • Primary Health Center (PHC) Level: Medical Officers (MOs), Staff Nurses, Pharmacists, and Lab Technicians

To maintain data integrity and workflow clarity, the app is governed by a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) System, which ensures that each user only accesses relevant modules and permissions based on their designation and facility level.

User Needs & Business Goals

  • Field users needed streamlined workflows tailored to their daily tasks.
  • Supervisors required visibility into data across levels without clutter.
  • Khushi Baby NGO aimed to ensure security, accuracy, and hierarchy alignment across users.

Research

Field Observations (Shadowing ASHAs and ANMs)

To understand the real-world context of CHIP usage, I conducted immersive field visits to rural areas. I shadowed ASHAs and ANMs during their daily routines, particularly while they:

  • Conducted household visits for DHS and other health-related data collection
  • Interacted with community members, often in challenging physical or social conditions
  • Used CHIP on low-end Android phones, sometimes with intermittent network or power access
