Problem Statement

Context & Background

The real estate sector relies heavily on Channel Partners (CPs), yet their workflows remain outdated and fragmented. Anarock recognized the need for a unified digital solution to enhance efficiency, trust, and scalability for both individual brokers and organizations.

  • CPs traditionally use scattered tools and offline methods to manage clients, listings, and commissions.
  • These manual processes result in inefficiencies, lost opportunities, and a lack of professional credibility.
  • There’s no single platform tailored to the complete journey of a CP—from onboarding to post-sale finance tracking.
  • Clients often hesitate to trust brokers due to unverified credentials and inconsistent communication.
  • Anarock aimed to bridge this gap by introducing a mobile-first platform to streamline and professionalize CP operations.

User Needs & Business Goals

To ensure the platform serves both Channel Partners and the business effectively, it was essential to align user needs with strategic business goals. The design focuses on solving day-to-day broker challenges while supporting Anarock's vision for scalable growth and trust-driven sales.

User Needs:

  • A simple onboarding flow for both individual CPs and organizations, including verification (e.g., RERA details).
  • A trusted identity tool (Digital Visiting Card) to build credibility with clients.
  • An easy-to-use system to browse, filter, and share property listings with clients.
  • A centralized space to manage leads, track client progress, and monitor commissions.

Business Goals:

  • Digitize the broker ecosystem to increase sales efficiency and reduce lead leakage.
  • Promote verified CPs to build trust and strengthen Anarock’s brand reputation.
  • Provide CPs with incentives like early commissions to encourage engagement and loyalty.
  • Enable scalability by supporting both individual and organizational users within one platform..

Metric is quantitative data that you collect & analyze and, hopefully, make better decisions based on this analysis.

Data Table

Data tables display information in a grid-like format of rows and columns. They organize information in a way that's easy to scan so that users can look for patterns and develop insights from data.

Side Navigation

Side navigation lets users navigate the entire content of a product or a section. These can be used for a single level or a multi-level navigation.

Filters

Filters provide a set of controls to reduce items in a collection based on attributes the user is interested in (e.g., filtering items by creation date). Filters can provide the following functionality: A list of interesting attributes common to items in a collection, and possible values to filter by.

Lists in web/mobile app UI design are galleries of text, images, or card options that can help users choose one or more options to achieve their goals effortlessly. Many common elements such as drop-down menus, list boxes, checkboxes, and radio button groups are typical examples of list UI design.
