Invest Application UI Casestudy

Bridging Founders and Funders for the Startups of Tomorrow.

Problem Statement

Startups often face significant challenges in securing the funding they need to scale, while potential investors struggle to find emerging ventures that align with their interests and investment goals. The current landscape lacks a centralized, user-friendly platform that connects startups seeking funding with investors looking for new opportunities. Startups need a seamless way to present their ideas, progress, and funding requirements, while investors require an intuitive interface to discover, evaluate, and invest in promising ventures.

The goal of this project is to design an app that bridges this gap, allowing startups to easily post their projects for potential investors to see, and enabling investors to browse, evaluate, and invest in various startups from a single platform. The design should focus on a streamlined user experience, transparency, and fostering trust between both parties.

Flows and UI Designs

Overview of the Final Design

The app is designed to offer a role-based, user-friendly experience for startup founders and investors, ensuring clarity and engagement.

  • Onboarding: Tailored for founders and investors, with social login and quick-start guides to reduce friction.
  • Homepage: Auto-adjusts based on user role, presenting curated lists for investors and management tools for founders.
  • Startup Cards: Investors can view key details and access full startup profiles with financial data and pitch decks.
  • Profile Section: Comprehensive profiles for both investors and founders, with networking features like direct messaging and meeting scheduling.
  • RBAC: Role-based access ensures users only see relevant information, maintaining security and personalization.

Onboarding

The onboarding process introduces users to the app's purpose and functionality, guiding both startup founders and potential investors through tailored experiences. For founders, the onboarding highlights steps to post their startup, while for investors, it explains how to browse and evaluate startups for funding. The design must ensure clarity, reduce friction, and set expectations, using a combination of visuals and short descriptions to ease users into the platform. Features like social login and quick-start guides could help reduce onboarding time and improve user retention.

Homepage

The homepage serves as the primary interface for investors, presenting a curated list of startups seeking funding. Startups are displayed in a card-based or list format, each featuring key information such as the startup name, industry, funding progress, and a brief description. Investors can easily browse, filter, and search for startups based on categories such as industry, funding stage, or location.

Each startup card provides a quick overview with the option to tap for more detailed information. Upon selection, users can view comprehensive startup profiles, including founders' details, pitch decks, current investors, and financial projections. The goal is to create a seamless, informative experience that encourages discovery and engagement, while offering startups the opportunity to showcase their potential.

Profile

The profile section is essential for establishing credibility and trust within the app, allowing both investors and startup owners to showcase their backgrounds, expertise, and objectives. The investor profile provides a comprehensive view of their investment history, interests, and areas of expertise. It includes personal information such as the investor's name, profile picture, and a brief bio highlighting their professional background and investment philosophy. Additionally, the profile features a list of past investments, detailing the startup names, amounts invested, and the outcomes of those investments, as well as a section for preferred industries to aid in personalized startup recommendations. Connection options for direct messaging or scheduling meetings with startup owners promote networking and collaboration.

Conclusion

Working on the Invest iOS Application was an enlightening experience that deepened my understanding of fintech and investment ecosystems. This project challenged me to design a platform that serves two distinct user groupsâ€”startup founders seeking funding and investors looking for promising opportunities. The key challenge was creating a seamless experience that builds trust and transparency between these parties while maintaining the security and credibility essential in financial transactions.

The role-based design approach proved crucial in addressing the different needs and workflows of founders and investors. By implementing tailored onboarding experiences, personalized dashboards, and comprehensive profile systems, I was able to create a platform that feels intuitive for both user types. The project reinforced the importance of user research in fintech applications, where trust and credibility are paramount. The experience highlighted how thoughtful UX design can bridge complex financial processes and make investment opportunities more accessible to both parties.
