Samasta Application & Dashboard

Transforming Primary Healthcare Delivery with Digital Efficiency and Data-Driven Insights

Project Overview

The goal was to design a mobile application for Health and Wellness Centre (HWC) doctors and staff in Karnataka to manage Outpatient Department (OPD) services efficiently. The app aimed to streamline patient registration, appointment scheduling, EMR management, telemedicine, health monitoring, inventory management, outreach activities, and provide a dashboard for higher officials.

Project type: End-to-end app + branding

Industry

Public Health Digitalisation

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Jira, Confluence & Google Meet

Duration

May 23 - Feb 24

Roles and Responsibilities

Led the entire design process across modules and user levels—translating complex workflows into intuitive, role-sensitive experiences.

Problem Statement

Primary healthcare facilities often struggle with manual processes, unstructured patient flow, and fragmented data systems. These issues lead to long wait times, inconsistent patient records, stock mismanagement, and poor visibility into community outreach efforts. Higher-level officials face challenges in accessing real-time data, monitoring health trends, and making informed decisions due to the lack of integrated reporting and oversight tools.

Context & Background

In many government health facilities, especially at the PHC and sub-center level, service delivery is burdened by manual workflows, paper-based record-keeping, and fragmented systems. These inefficiencies lead to long wait times, poor tracking of patient care, delayed interventions, and frequent shortages of essential medicines. Additionally, field activities such as immunization drives and follow-ups often go undocumented or are tracked inconsistently. The absence of real-time insights also hinders program planning at the district or state level.

User Needs & Business Goals

User Needs:

  • Health workers require a streamlined way to manage patient queues, reduce bottlenecks, and ensure smooth service transitions across OPD, lab, and pharmacy.
  • ANMs and ASHAs need tools to register patients quickly, maintain accurate records, and plan outreach visits efficiently.
  • Pharmacists and store managers need up-to-date stock information and alerts to avoid medicine shortages.
  • Medical Officers need visibility into patient flow, medicine use, and disease trends to make timely decisions.
  • Program managers and higher officials require aggregated data to monitor facility performance and guide policy interventions.

Business Goals:

  • Digitize health service workflows to improve operational efficiency and data accuracy.
  • Reduce patient wait times and optimize resource usage.
  • Ensure continuous availability of essential medicines and supplies.
  • Strengthen accountability in community outreach activities.
  • Enable data-driven planning and performance monitoring across health programs.

Userflow

The application's information architecture was redesigned based on user feedback and persona insights. The goal was to ensure that navigation was intuitive, and information was organized in a logical and accessible manner.

Final Solution

Overview of the Final Design

The final design of Samasta is a modular digital health system tailored for Primary Health Centers (PHCs), aiming to enhance the delivery and monitoring of outpatient services. The solution includes five key modules—Out Patient Department (OPD), Inventory Management, Patient Registration, Outreach Activity, and Dashboard—each optimized to support health workers and officials in delivering timely, efficient, and data-driven care.

  • The OPD module integrates queue management and patient tracking to streamline the entire consultation and treatment workflow.
  • Inventory Management ensures uninterrupted availability of medicines and equipment through real-time stock updates and automated restocking alerts.
  • The Patient Registration system simplifies intake with autofill forms and Aadhaar integration for faster verifications.
  • The Outreach Activity module helps plan, log, and follow up on community-based health programs.
  • Finally, a centralized Dashboard enables higher officials to monitor service delivery, analyze trends, and make informed decisions for resource planning.

Out Patient Department (OPD)

This module handles the entire OPD workflow, from patient arrival to consultation and treatment. It integrates queue management, displaying patient status and prioritizing cases based on urgency. Health workers can manage patient flow, assign tokens, and streamline movement between different service points (e.g., consultation, lab tests, pharmacy). The system tracks patients' progress through the OPD process, ensuring efficient and timely care.

Inventory Management

Helps manage medical supplies and resources efficiently. This module tracks stock levels of medicines, vaccines, and equipment in real time, sends alerts for low stock, and generates restocking orders. It also maintains a history of usage, expiration dates, and supply chain updates.

Patient Registration

Streamlines patient intake by allowing health workers to efficiently capture patient details, demographic information, and medical history. This module offers a user-friendly form with auto-fill features, reducing data entry time and ensuring accuracy. Integrated with Aadhaar and other identification systems for quick verifications.

Outreach activity

Enables health workers to plan and track community outreach initiatives. This module includes features for scheduling immunization drives, health camps, and follow-up visits, with integrated reminders. It also logs outreach efforts, records attendance, and syncs data with centralized health records to ensure continuity of care.

Dashboard

A centralized hub for higher-level officials to monitor OPD performance, track patient visits, and view trends in disease outbreaks and medication prescriptions. This module provides data visualization through charts and graphs, giving insights into resource allocation, patient demographics, and the effectiveness of interventions.

Conclusion

Working on the HWC OPD management app was an insightful journey, allowing me to design a system that significantly improves healthcare service delivery in rural areas. By focusing on user-centered design, I created modules that simplify complex workflows for health workers and ensure better patient care through streamlined processes. Integrating queue management with the OPD workflow, inventory management, and outreach activities provided a comprehensive solution that caters to the diverse needs of health workers and administrative officials alike.

This project deepened my understanding of healthcare challenges in underserved areas and the role of efficient digital solutions in addressing them. The key takeaway was striking a balance between usability and functionality—designing for users with varying technical expertise while ensuring all critical operations are handled efficiently. The experience reinforced the importance of aligning technology with the needs of healthcare systems and inspired me to continue exploring ways to create impactful designs in the health sector.
