UX Animation

Streamlining Healthcare Operations at the Grassroots.

Project Overview

UI animation is the process of adding motion to UI elements in order to enhance a product's interactivity. UX and UI designers use animation to guide them around the interface, alert users of a change, influence users' decisions, and indicate a relationship between elements—among other uses. UI animation also reduces the mechanical feel of a website or app, creating a much more natural and intuitive experience.

Project type: End-to-end app + branding

Industry

Public Health Digitalisation

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Jira, Confluence & Google Meet

Duration

Roles and Responsibilities

Led the entire design process across modules and user levels—translating complex workflows into intuitive, role-sensitive experiences.

Problem Statement

In a rapidly evolving digital landscape, organizations face challenges in maintaining visual consistency and user experience across multiple platforms and products. Existing design processes often lead to fragmented design elements, inefficient collaboration, and a lack of accessibility for both designers and developers. TARP aims to address these issues by providing a comprehensive design system that encompasses a cohesive set of guidelines, components, and best practices tailored for scalability and flexibility. By streamlining workflows and ensuring consistency in design and branding, TARP empowers teams to create intuitive, user-friendly interfaces that enhance overall user engagement and satisfaction.

Userflow

The application's information architecture was redesigned based on user feedback and persona insights. The goal was to ensure that navigation was intuitive, and information was organized in a logical and accessible manner.

Overview of the Final Design

The CHIP application was designed to be role-sensitive, offline-capable, and workflow-driven — addressing real-life needs of ASHAs, ANMs, CHOs, and PHC-level staff across rural healthcare settings.

  • Homepage and navigation auto-adjust based on the user's role and facility level.
  • Punch-IN reflects GPS and timestamp clearly.
  • RBAC implemented via user profile at login and governed across UI components.

Animation 1

The onboarding process introduces users to the app’s purpose and functionality, guiding both startup founders and potential investors through tailored experiences. For founders, the onboarding highlights steps to post their startup, while for investors, it explains how to browse and evaluate startups for funding. The design must ensure clarity, reduce friction, and set expectations, using a combination of visuals and short descriptions to ease users into the platform. Features like social login and quick-start guides could help reduce onboarding time and improve user retention.

Animation 2

The homepage serves as the primary interface for investors, presenting a curated list of startups seeking funding. Startups are displayed in a card-based or list format, each featuring key information such as the startup name, industry, funding progress, and a brief description. Investors can easily browse, filter, and search for startups based on categories such as industry, funding stage, or location.

Animation 3

The profile section is essential for establishing credibility and trust within the app, allowing both investors and startup owners to showcase their backgrounds, expertise, and objectives. The investor profile provides a comprehensive view of their investment history, interests, and areas of expertise. It includes personal information such as the investor's name, profile picture, and a brief bio highlighting their professional background and investment philosophy. Additionally, the profile features a list of past investments, detailing the startup names, amounts invested, and the outcomes of those investments, as well as a section for preferred industries to aid in personalized startup recommendations. Connection options for direct messaging or scheduling meetings with startup owners promote networking and collaboration.

Conclusion

Working on the HWC OPD management app was an insightful journey, allowing me to design a system that significantly improves healthcare service delivery in rural areas. By focusing on user-centered design, I created modules that simplify complex workflows for health workers and ensure better patient care through streamlined processes. Integrating queue management with the OPD workflow, inventory management, and outreach activities provided a comprehensive solution that caters to the diverse needs of health workers and administrative officials alike.

This project deepened my understanding of healthcare challenges in underserved areas and the role of efficient digital solutions in addressing them. The key takeaway was striking a balance between usability and functionality—designing for users with varying technical expertise while ensuring all critical operations are handled efficiently. The experience reinforced the importance of aligning technology with the needs of healthcare systems and inspired me to continue exploring ways to create impactful designs in the health sector.
